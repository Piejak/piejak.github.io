Nikola Tesla

Mad Scientist

Tesla's Timeline
  • 1856 - Born in modern-day Croatia
  • 1875 - Enrolls in Austrian Polytechnic after nearly being drafted
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest to work as chief electrician at the Budapest Telephone Exchange, making many improvements to the equiptment while he was there
  • 1884 - Emigrated to New York City where he was hired by Thomas Edison for simple electrical engineering work, but this quickly escalated to difficult problem solving
  • 1886 - By this point Tesla had left Edison to work on his own ventures. Funded by Alfred S. Brown of Western Union, Tesla formed Tesla Electric Company where he developed a motor that ran on alternating current.
